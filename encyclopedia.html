acoustic genres

› singer-songwriter

with less emphasis on instrumental accompaniment, focuses on the writing, emotion and storytelling of the musician's lyrics, rooted in folk music

› indie folk

alternative genre of music that blends indie rock and folk influences

› ethereal folk

dreamy, underwater, floaty subgenre of indie folk

› chamber folk

style of typically gentle fingerpicked acoustic guitar or harp-led music

› botanica

botanica music is a genre that combines nostalgia-tinged timbres from acoustic instrumentation like piano, strings, guitars, and vocals with spectral processing and glitch elements

› chamber pop

genre that combines rock music with the intricate use of strings, horns, piano, and vocal harmonies, and other components drawn from the orchestral and lounge pop of the 1960s, with an emphasis on melody and texture

› folktronica

genre of indie electronic music comprising elements of folk music and electronica, often using stringed instruments & delicate, textural sound design

› indie rock

genre characterized by independent, non-mainstream artists creating music that maintains pop based song writing styles whilst also using the creative freedom present to branch out into more unique musical ideas

› shoegaze

subgenre of indie and alternative rock characterized by its ethereal mixture of obscured vocals, guitar distortion and effects, feedback, and overwhelming volume

› post-rock

subgenre of experimental rock that emphasizes texture, atmosphere, and non-traditional song structures over conventional rock techniques

› slowcore

subgenre of indie rock characterised by its subdued tempos, minimalist instrumentation, and sombre vocal performances

› noise rock

noise-oriented style of experimental rock that spun off from punk rock in the 1980s • drawing on movements such as minimalism, artists indulge in extreme distortion through electric guitars and electronic instrumentation

› art rock

subgenre of rock music that generally reflects a challenging or avant-garde approach to rock, or which makes use of modernist, experimental, or unconventional elements

› post-hardcore

punk rock music genre that maintains the aggression and intensity of hardcore punk but emphasizes a greater degree of creative expression

› metalcore

subgenre of heavy metal with slow intense breakdowns, screaming vocals and heavy riffs, as well as double bass drumming

jazz rooted genres

› bossa nova

relaxed style of samba • calm syncopated rhythm, unconventional chords, and influences from jazz and classical music

› jazz

swing and blue notes, complex chords, call and response vocals, polyrhythms, improvisation, all in one darn good acoustic cake

› neo-soul

less conventional sound, heavily based on soul music

atmospheric genres

› microtonal harp / experimental violin

what i wanna call leya next to chamber folk, alternate tunings on harp and violin that make harsh soundscapes

› ambient

genre of music that emphasizes tone and atmosphere over traditional musical structure or rhythm • ambient music uses textural layers of sound that can reward both passive and active listening

› vaporwave

defined partly by slowed-down, chopped and screwed samples of smooth jazz, 1970s elevator music, r&b, and lounge music

textural genres

› electronica

broad group of electronic-based music styles intended for active listening rather than strictly for dancing

› minimal techno

subgenre of techno, stripped-down aesthetic that exploits repetition and understated development

› future garage

genre of electronic music that incorporates a variety of influences from garage • pitched vocal chops, atmosphere, resse basses

› stutter house

subgenre of electronic music that features a stuttering or pulsating vocal, lead, or synth pad

› uk bass

club music that emerged in the United Kingdom during the mid-2000s under the influence of diverse genres such as house, grime, dubstep, future garage and r&b

pop facing genres

› garage / uk garage

percussive, shuffled rhythms with syncopated hi-hats, cymbals, and snares • commonly features 'chopped up' and time-stretched or happily pitch-shifted vocal samples

› edm pop

dancy electronic music paired with pop elements, usually structure and singing styles

› indie pop

genre and subculture that combines guitar pop with a diy ethic in opposition to the style and tone of mainstream pop music

› hyperpop

maximalist pop and avant-garde mix with electronic, rock and hip hop influences
